# Solar Energy Fundamentals

## 1. Solar Irradiance

Solar irradiance is the power per unit area received from the Sun. It varies by location, season, and time of day.

### Key Metrics
| Metric | Definition | Typical Value (mid-latitude) |
|---|---|---|
| Peak Sun Hours (PSH) | Hours of 1000 W/m² equivalent | 3.5–5.5 h/day (annual avg) |
| Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) | Total solar energy on flat surface | 1,000–2,000 kWh/m²/year |
| Direct Normal Irradiance (DNI) | Direct beam component | 60–80% of GHI |

### Seasonal Variation
- Summer vs. winter ratio: **3:1 to 5:1** at 50° latitude
- This creates the fundamental mismatch: maximum generation in summer, maximum demand in winter (heating-dominated climates)

## 2. Panel Efficiency

### Current Technology (2025)
| Technology | Lab Efficiency | Commercial Efficiency | Cost ($/Wp) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monocrystalline Si | 26.7% | 20–22% | $0.20–0.30 |
| Perovskite (emerging) | 33.7% (tandem) | 18–20% | $0.15–0.25 (projected) |
| Thin-film (CdTe) | 22.1% | 17–19% | $0.18–0.25 |

### Temperature Coefficient
- Silicon panels lose **0.3–0.5% efficiency per °C** above 25°C
- Hot climates: Effective loss of 5–15% during peak hours
- **Implication**: Panel output peaks in spring, not summer (moderate temperatures + good irradiance)

## 3. Degradation

- Annual degradation rate: **0.5–0.8%/year** for monocrystalline Si
- After 25 years: ~85% of original output remaining
- LID (Light-Induced Degradation): 1–3% loss in first year (then stabilizes)
- PID (Potential-Induced Degradation): Preventable with proper system design

## 4. Levelized Cost of Energy (LCOE)

```
LCOE = (Total Lifecycle Cost) / (Total Lifetime Energy Production)

Example:
- System cost: $1.00/Wp × 10 kWp = $10,000
- Annual production: 14,000 kWh (1,400 kWh/kWp at good site)
- Degradation: 0.6%/year
- O&M: $100/year
- Lifetime: 30 years
- Discount rate: 5%

LCOE ≈ $0.035–0.05/kWh (utility-scale, 2025)
```

Solar LCOE is now **below coal and gas** in most markets — but this is an *average* cost. The *marginal* value of solar drops as penetration increases (duck curve problem).
