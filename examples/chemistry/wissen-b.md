# Mechanical Properties & Processing of Biopolymers

## 1. Mechanical Properties Comparison

| Property | PLA | PHA (PHB) | PHBV | TPS | LDPE (reference) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tensile strength (MPa) | 50–70 | 25–40 | 20–35 | 5–15 | 8–25 |
| Elongation at break (%) | 2–10 | 3–5 | 5–15 | 20–100 | 100–600 |
| Young's modulus (GPa) | 3.0–4.0 | 3.0–3.5 | 0.8–2.0 | 0.1–0.5 | 0.1–0.3 |
| Glass transition Tg (°C) | 55–65 | 5–10 | -5 to 5 | -20 to 30 | -120 |
| Melting point Tm (°C) | 150–180 | 170–180 | 130–160 | N/A (amorphous) | 105–115 |

### Key Observations
- PLA is **stiff but brittle** (high modulus, low elongation)
- PHA is similarly brittle and degrades near its melting point
- TPS is flexible but weak and moisture-sensitive
- **No single biopolymer matches LDPE's combination** of flexibility, toughness, and barrier

## 2. Barrier Properties

| Material | O₂ Permeability (cc·mm/m²·day·atm) | Water Vapor Transmission (g·mm/m²·day) |
|---|---|---|
| PLA | 15–25 | 1.5–3.0 |
| PHB | 2–5 | 1.0–2.0 |
| TPS | 500–2000 | 50–200 |
| PET (reference) | 1–3 | 0.5–1.0 |
| LDPE (reference) | 50–200 | 0.5–1.5 |

**PHA has the best barrier** among biopolymers — approaching PET for oxygen. This makes it valuable for food packaging coatings.

## 3. Processing Methods

### Extrusion
- PLA: Processes well at 170–200°C, wide processing window
- PHA: Narrow window (170–180°C), degrades rapidly above 180°C
- TPS: Requires plasticizer, processes at 130–170°C

### Injection Molding
- PLA: Excellent — mold temperatures 25–55°C, cycle times comparable to PS
- PHA: Difficult — slow crystallization requires long cooling, cycle times **2–3x longer** than PLA
- TPS: Only as blend component (too weak alone)

### Crystallinity Control
- PLA crystallinity is tunable: **0–60%** depending on processing
  - Amorphous PLA: Transparent, lower heat resistance (HDT ~55°C)
  - Semi-crystalline PLA: Opaque, higher heat resistance (HDT ~120°C with nucleation)
- **Nucleating agents** (talc, LAK) accelerate crystallization → higher Tc, shorter cycle times
- **Trade-off**: Higher crystallinity improves heat resistance but reduces biodegradation rate (crystalline regions resist hydrolysis)
