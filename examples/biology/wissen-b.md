# Drug Resistance Mechanisms in Cancer

## 1. Drug Efflux Pumps

### ABC Transporters
ATP-binding cassette (ABC) transporters actively pump chemotherapy drugs out of tumor cells, reducing intracellular drug concentration below therapeutic thresholds.

| Transporter | Gene | Substrates | Clinical Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| P-glycoprotein (P-gp) | ABCB1/MDR1 | Taxanes, anthracyclines, vinca alkaloids | Most common multidrug resistance mechanism |
| MRP1 | ABCC1 | Methotrexate, etoposide, doxorubicin | Amplified in ~30% of resistant tumors |
| BCRP | ABCG2 | Mitoxantrone, topotecan, gefitinib | Enriched in cancer stem cells |

**Key finding**: P-gp expression is **upregulated by hypoxia** via HIF-1α, creating a direct link between tumor microenvironment and drug resistance.

## 2. Target Mutations

### Kinase Domain Mutations
Targeted therapies are vulnerable to point mutations in their binding site:

- **EGFR T790M** — confers resistance to first-generation EGFR inhibitors (gefitinib, erlotinib)
- **ALK L1196M** — resistance to crizotinib
- **BCR-ABL T315I** — resistance to imatinib

**Bypass signaling**: Even without target mutations, tumors can activate alternative pathways:
- EGFR blockade → MET amplification → continued signaling
- BRAF inhibition → CRAF-mediated reactivation of MAPK pathway

## 3. Pathway Rewiring

Tumors adapt to single-agent therapy by activating compensatory signaling cascades:

### PI3K/AKT/mTOR and RAS/MAPK Crosstalk
- Inhibiting PI3K → compensatory MAPK activation
- Inhibiting MAPK → compensatory PI3K activation
- **Implication**: Single-pathway inhibition often leads to rebound activation of the parallel pathway

### Epigenetic Adaptation
- Drug-tolerant persister cells survive initial treatment via epigenetic reprogramming
- These cells are not genetically resistant but phenotypically plastic
- Can revert to sensitive state after drug holiday (~2 weeks off treatment)
- Persistence mediated by KDM5A histone demethylase activity

## 4. Stem Cell Resistance

Cancer stem cells (CSCs) are intrinsically resistant to most conventional therapies:
- Quiescent (slow-cycling) → chemotherapy targets dividing cells
- High expression of ABC transporters
- Enhanced DNA repair mechanisms
- Niche protection from immune attack

**Therapeutic implication**: Effective treatment must target both bulk tumor AND cancer stem cells.
