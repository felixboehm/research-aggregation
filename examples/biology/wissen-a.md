# Tumor Microenvironment

## 1. Hypoxia

Solid tumors outgrow their blood supply, creating regions with oxygen levels below **1–2% O₂** (vs. ~5% in normal tissue). Hypoxia triggers a cascade of adaptive responses:

### Hypoxia-Inducible Factor (HIF-1α)
- Master regulator of hypoxic response
- Upregulates VEGF (angiogenesis), GLUT1 (glucose uptake), and drug efflux pumps (MDR1/P-glycoprotein)
- Stabilized when O₂ < 2% — normally degraded by VHL-mediated ubiquitination

### Consequences for Treatment
- Hypoxic cells are **3x more resistant** to radiation therapy (oxygen fixation hypothesis)
- Chemotherapy delivery is impaired — drugs cannot reach poorly vascularized regions
- Hypoxia selects for aggressive, therapy-resistant clones

## 2. Immune Evasion

Tumors develop multiple mechanisms to escape immune detection:

| Mechanism | Effect | Key Molecule |
|---|---|---|
| PD-L1 upregulation | T-cell exhaustion | PD-1/PD-L1 axis |
| Treg recruitment | Immune suppression | TGF-β, IL-10 |
| MHC-I downregulation | Antigen presentation loss | β2-microglobulin |
| IDO expression | Tryptophan depletion → T-cell dysfunction | Indoleamine 2,3-dioxygenase |

**Hypoxia amplifies immune evasion**: HIF-1α directly upregulates PD-L1 expression and promotes Treg infiltration.

## 3. Angiogenesis

Tumor vasculature is structurally abnormal — leaky, tortuous, and poorly organized.

### VEGF Signaling
- VEGF-A is the primary driver of tumor angiogenesis
- Secreted by tumor cells (especially under hypoxia) and stromal cells
- Binds VEGFR-2 on endothelial cells → proliferation, migration, survival

### Vascular Normalization Hypothesis (Jain, 2001)
Anti-angiogenic therapy at **moderate doses** can temporarily normalize tumor vasculature:
- Reduced vessel leakiness → improved drug delivery
- Better oxygenation → improved radiation sensitivity
- "Normalization window" lasts approximately **1–2 weeks** after anti-VEGF treatment

**Clinical implication**: Timing of combination therapy is critical — other treatments should be administered during the normalization window.
